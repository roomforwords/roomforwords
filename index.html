About the Author

Shashank

Writing as The Unsaid Tone

Shashank is the voice behind RoomForWords — a space built for thoughts that refused to stay quiet. He writes not to explain love, but to document it. Not to teach, but to let emotion exist without correction.

His debut collection, Why I Was Begging in Love, was written during the months after heartbreak — from sleepless nights where memories replayed without permission, and a heart that kept asking questions the mind could never answer.

These books are not a lesson. They are proof. Proof that he loved. That is all they need to be.

"She was a flower on the plant,"
"and I was just a boy who didn't even know"
"the world could hold such beauty."
"I saw her one afternoon"
"and kept looking at her with eyes full of love,"
"unaware of how fragile that beauty was."
"By evening, in my fear of losing her,"
"I plucked her away with my own hands."
"Now she lives only in my memories—"
"a flower pressed forever"
"between the pages of my diary."
